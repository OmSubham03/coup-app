📜 Coup Rules

Overview

Each player starts with 2 coins and 2 hidden influence cards. Last player with influence wins.

Actions (anyone can take)

  • Income — Take 1 coin
  • Foreign Aid — Take 2 coins (blockable by Duke)
  • Coup — Pay 7 coins, target loses 1 influence (mandatory at 10+)

Character Actions

  • Duke — Tax: Take 3 coins. Blocks Foreign Aid
  • Assassin — Assassinate: Pay 3 coins, target loses influence (blockable by Contessa)
  • Captain — Steal: Take 2 coins from target (blockable by Captain/Ambassador)
  • Ambassador — Exchange: Draw 2 cards, keep what you want. Blocks stealing
  • Contessa — Blocks assassination

Inquisitor Variant

  • Inquisitor — Exchange: Draw 1 card, keep what you want. Can interrogate a player to peek at a card and optionally force them to swap it. Blocks stealing

Bluffing & Challenges

You can claim any character action regardless of your cards. Any player can challenge a claim. If the challenge succeeds (bluff caught), the liar loses influence. If the challenge fails, the challenger loses influence and the claimer swaps their revealed card.

Blocking

Certain actions can be blocked by claiming a counter-character. Blocks can also be challenged.

🃏 Texas Hold'em Rules

Overview

Each player is dealt 2 hole cards face down. 5 community cards are dealt face up in stages. Make the best 5-card hand from any combination of your 2 hole cards and the 5 community cards.

Betting Rounds

  • Pre-flop — After hole cards are dealt. Small blind and big blind are posted.
  • Flop — 3 community cards are dealt face up.
  • Turn — 1 more community card is dealt.
  • River — Final community card is dealt.
  • Showdown — Remaining players reveal hands. Best hand wins the pot.

Actions

  • Fold — Give up your hand and forfeit bets.
  • Check — Pass without betting (only if no bet to match).
  • Call — Match the current bet.
  • Raise — Increase the current bet.
  • All In — Bet all remaining chips.

Hand Rankings (highest to lowest)

  • Royal Flush — A, K, Q, J, 10 of the same suit
  • Straight Flush — Five consecutive cards of the same suit
  • Four of a Kind — Four cards of the same rank
  • Full House — Three of a kind + a pair
  • Flush — Five cards of the same suit
  • Straight — Five consecutive cards
  • Three of a Kind — Three cards of the same rank
  • Two Pair — Two different pairs
  • One Pair — Two cards of the same rank
  • High Card — Highest card plays

Blinds

The player to the left of the dealer posts the small blind. The next player posts the big blind (2x small blind). The dealer button rotates clockwise each hand.

🎲 Ludo Rules

Objective

Be the first player to move all 4 tokens from your yard, around the board, and into your home column.

Setup

  • 2-4 players, each with 4 tokens starting in their yard.
  • For 2 players, players sit opposite each other on the board.

Rolling & Moving

  • Roll a 6 to move a token from your yard onto the board.
  • Move any active token forward by the dice value.
  • Rolling a 6 gives you a bonus roll.
  • Three consecutive 6s = your turn is lost.

Capturing

  • Landing on an opponent's token sends it back to their yard.
  • Capturing gives you a bonus roll.
  • Tokens on ★ safe squares (star positions) cannot be captured.

Home Column

  • After circling the board, tokens enter the home column (6 squares).
  • You need an exact roll to reach the final home position.
  • Tokens in the home column are safe from opponents.

Winning

The first player to get all 4 tokens into the home column wins!

🃁 29 Card Game Rules

Overview

29 is a 4-player partnership trick-taking game popular in South Asia. Partners sit opposite each other. The goal is to win tricks containing valuable cards.

Cards & Points

  • 32 cards used: J, 9, A, 10, K, Q, 8, 7 of each suit
  • Ranking (high→low): J → 9 → A → 10 → K → Q → 8 → 7
  • J = 3pts, 9 = 2pts, A = 1pt, 10 = 1pt, K/Q/8/7 = 0pts
  • Total: 28 card points + 1 for last trick = 29

Bidding

  • 4 cards are dealt to each player first.
  • Players bid (15-28) for the right to choose trump. Highest bidder wins.
  • The bidder secretly picks a trump suit (hidden from others).
  • Then 4 more cards are dealt to each player (8 total).

Playing Tricks

  • You must follow suit if you can.
  • Trump is hidden initially. The first player unable to follow suit can ask for trump reveal.
  • After trump is revealed, trumps beat all other suits.
  • If you trump, next player must over-trump if possible.
  • Winner of each trick leads the next.

Pair (K+Q of Trump)

  • After trump is revealed, if you hold both King and Queen of trumps, you can declare a Pair.
  • Bidding team's pair: reduces target by 4 (min 15).
  • Opponent's pair: increases target by 4 (max 28).

Scoring

  • If bidding team meets their bid → they gain 1 game point.
  • If they fail → they lose 1 game point (2 if under half the bid).
  • First team to +6 wins. A team at -6 loses.

❤️ Hearts Rules

Overview

Hearts is a 4-player trick-avoidance game. The goal is to have the lowest score when any player reaches 100 points.

Cards & Points

  • Standard 52-card deck, 13 cards per player
  • Each ♥ Heart = 1 point
  • Q♠ Queen of Spades = 13 points
  • Total possible per hand: 26 points

Passing

  • Before each hand, pass 3 cards to another player
  • Rotation: Left → Right → Across → No Pass (repeats)

Playing

  • Player with 2♣ leads the first trick
  • Must follow suit if possible
  • No hearts or Q♠ on the first trick
  • Hearts can't be led until a heart has been played (broken)
  • Highest card of led suit wins the trick (no trumps)

Shooting the Moon 🌙

If one player takes all 26 penalty points (all hearts + Q♠), they score 0 and everyone else gets +26!

Winning

Game ends when someone hits 100 points. Player with the lowest score wins!

🫱 Commune Rules

Overview

A bluffing card game using a standard 52-card deck where 2s are wild. Two community cards are dealt face-up for all to see. Players declare poker-style hands based on the community cards plus their own hand. Each declaration must be higher than the last — or you can call the bluff!

Setup

  • Each player starts with 5 tokens (lives).
  • 2 community cards are dealt face-up each round.
  • Each player's hand = 6 minus tokens lost cards (minimum 1).
  • A fresh shuffled deck is dealt each round.

Wild Cards

  • 2s are wild — they can count as any rank or suit.

On Your Turn

  • Declare — Claim a poker hand exists in the community cards + your own hand. Must be higher than the previous declaration.
  • Call — Challenge the previous player's declaration (only if someone already declared).

Hand Rankings (lowest to highest)

  • High Card — Any single rank
  • Pair — Two of the same rank
  • Two Pair — Two different pairs
  • Three of a Kind — Three of the same rank
  • Straight — Five consecutive ranks
  • Full House — Three of a kind + a pair
  • Four of a Kind — Four of the same rank
  • Two Trips — Two sets of three of a kind
  • Ulta Straight — All ranks from 3 to Ace (12 cards)
  • Four + Three — Four of a kind + three of a kind
  • Five of a Kind — Five of the same rank (using wilds)

Calling

When you call, all cards are revealed. The declared hand is checked against ALL dealt cards — community cards and every player's hand (using wilds). If the hand IS present, the caller loses a token. If it's NOT present, the declarer loses a token.

Elimination

Lose all 5 tokens and you're out. As you lose tokens, you receive fewer cards. Last player standing wins!

❓ 20 Questions Rules

Overview

One player (the Word-Giver) picks a secret word. Everyone else has 20 yes/no questions to figure out what it is!

Setup

  • The Word-Giver chooses a category (Name, Place, Animal, or Thing) and enters a secret word.
  • The category is revealed to all players, but the word stays hidden.

Asking Questions

  • Players take turns asking one question at a time.
  • Questions should be yes/no style (e.g., "Is it alive?").
  • The Word-Giver answers Yes, No, or can type a short comment.

Making a Guess

  • Any guesser can make a guess at any time — no need to wait for your turn!
  • Each guess costs a question, so guess wisely.
  • The Word-Giver manually verifies each guess as correct or wrong.
  • If correct, the game ends and everyone sees both the guess and the secret word.

Final Guess Round

  • After all 20 questions are used up, each guesser gets one final guess.
  • Players guess one at a time in turn order.
  • The Word-Giver verifies each final guess.
  • If anyone guesses correctly, the guessers win!

Winning

  • Guessers win if anyone guesses the word correctly (during questions or final guess).
  • Word-Giver wins if nobody guesses the word.

Next Round

After the game ends, the Word-Giver role rotates to the next player, and a new round begins!
